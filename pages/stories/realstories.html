Real Stories: The Water Crisis in Myanmar

The Lifeline of Our Planet

Water is more than a resource it life. From the tiniest leaf to the largest animal, nothing exists without it. But in Myanmar’s rural regions, clean water is becoming harder to find.

In villages across the dry zones of Mandalay and Magway, families walk hours to collect water that is often unsafe. Wells dry up, reservoirs shrink, and hope dwindles. It’s a daily struggle, especially for women and children.

We used to have rain Aungs Story

Aung, a farmer from Minbu Township, recalls a time when rainfall was reliable. Now, the rains come late — or not at all. “We dig deeper every year,” he says. “But water is disappearing. We can't grow enough rice to feed our family.”

His story is one of many, but it's a reminder: conserving water isn't just global. It's personal, immediate, and local.
