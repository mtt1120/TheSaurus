Why is conserving water important?

Conserving water is important because it makes sure that there is enough clean water for the future and current generation. With the population growing and the demand increasing, getting access to clean water is getting harder and harder. Conserving water has a lot of benefits ranging from protecting the limited resourve to preserving ecosystems. Fresh water is a very rare resource but people are wasting it.

1

Water is finite, only 3% of the water on earth is fresh water, and most of it is blocked in ice or underground, which leaves us with only a fraction if that we can access.

2

Every single life depends on water, it includes plants, humans and ecosystems, and without water, we cannot live. With no water, it will be the end of life on earth.

3

Conserving water makes sure that rivers, lakes and wetlands live which supports wild life and preserve nature. Many rivers and lakes are drying up and marine life is vanishing.
