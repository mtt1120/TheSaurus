Pratical tips

Bathroom Conservation

There are many ways to conserve water inside the bathroom.

  • Turn off the tap while brushing teeth
  • Fix any leaking faucets to prevent water waste
  • Shower instead of bathing
  • Apply aerators

Combat Showers

Combat showers are used by NAVY seals to save water and time. Here's how you can too:

  • Turn on the shower and get wet
  • Turn off and lather with soap
  • Turn the shower back on and rinse off soap.

Kitchen Conservation

The kitchen is one of the places that has a lot of potential for conserving water.

  • If you use a dishwasher, only run it when the load is full.
  • When manually washing the dishes, rinse smaller items with a spray bottle.
  • Reduce the amount of dishes need for cleaning. Assign minimal utensils and plates.
  • As in the bathroom, apply aerators to reduce flow but not efficiency.

General Tips

One way to promote water conservation is through personal water metering. Measuring the amount of water you use per day makes you aware of the actions and reducing usage easier. What gets measured gets changed. Another effective water conservation method is to combine behavioral changes with technological solutions, including installing energy-efficient water heads, faucet aerators, rainwater harvesting systems, and automatic faucets. To combat water shortages, store the water you would have normally used in containers. These conservation practices will help adapt to climate change while preserving water resources.

Small habits you can apply

There are so many things you can do as an individual in the community to help conserve water even without the plans of the society. Learn how to save water one drop at a time.
